In the first unit of 7th grade math, there are four main learning targets our class will explore.

For each learning target, there will be a glossary of terms, videos, images of worked examples, and online practice.

Learning Target 1: I am showing a number and its opposite have a sum of zero.

This means that you can show positive and negative quantities have opposite directions on a number line, determine additive inverses, show absolute value of a rational number on a number line, and understand absolute value as distance.

Learning Target 2: I am adding and subtracting rational numbers.

This means that you can add and subtract integers, decimals, and fractions. You can also model addition and subtraction on a number line.

Learning Target 3: I am multiplying and dividing rational numbers.

This means that you can multiply and divide integers, decimals, and fractions. You can represent multiplication and division using multiple strategies.

Learning Target 4: I am converting rational numbers

This means that you can convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages. You can determine if a decimal terminates or repeats. You can solve multi-step word problems by converting between forms.
